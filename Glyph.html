Glyph Lab

The lab uses data sources such as Natural Earth, World Happiness Report 2018, and Climate Weather Surface of Brazil. The lab objectives were to use the ArcGIS Charts to renderer to visualize the world happiness data parts-of-whole and multivariate data, perform basic data matching of geographic data fields, use Python to read GIS attribute tables, use proportional or graduated symbol maps and multiple layers to encode multiple attributes.

Figure 1: Maps of happiness measures

Figure 1 illustrates maps of Europe which display bar and pie charts representing the factors influencing happiness in each country. These factors were normalized based on the population of each respective country. The variable of social support, defined as the national average of responses (o or 1) to the question "If you were in trouble, do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you whenever you need them, or not?" was found to be the highest influencing factor on happiness for most countries. GDP and healthy life followed as the next significant factors. In contrast, freedom, generosity, and the perspective of corruption were all similarly ranked at a comparable level, as observed across most countries.

Figure 2: A proportional symbol map of Denver Zoo trees

>

Figure 2 displays a proportional symbol map of the Denver Zoo trees as a percentage of the total number of trees. The symbol used to represent the trees is color-coded in green. The map is centered on the location of the Denver Zoo, and a smaller map is provided to indicate the location of the zoo in the broader context of the US. The map provides a visual representation and density of trees within the zoo, allowing for easy comparison between different areas.

Figure 3: Central West Map

>

Figure 3 portrays the geographic distribution of relative humidity, maximum temperature, and atmospheric pressure in Brazil. The circle symbols on the map represent the relative humidity levels, where smaller circles denote lower relative humidity. It is noteworthy that all the locations included in the map have moderate relative humidity, and no specific area is more humid than the others. The colors on the map indicate the maximum temperature values, with most areas displaying maximum temperatures randing from 19 to 31 degress Celsius. Moreover, the figure highlights the location with the highest atmospheric pressure, represented by a blue-cyan color. By providing information about multiple climatic variables across Brazil, this figure can aid in understanding of the local weather patterns.
