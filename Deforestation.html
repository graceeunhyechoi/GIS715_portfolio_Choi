Categorizing fires by their causes in South America from 2001 to 2019

I have created general-purpose maps at two different scales: one for the entire South America and another specifically for Colombia, both displaying the results of fire classification. To ensure clarity and intuition in the classification maps, I applied the techniques I learned in class, such as using colors that best represent the data. For instance, pixels that require further classification are represented by black while fires that have been classified are depicted in red. Similarly, for the fire classification map for South America, I used distinct colors to make the information as clear as possible. Overall, the maps present a detailed analysis of fire classification in South America and Colombia through five informative figures.

Figure 1:Andela et al. (2022)'s method

Figure 1 depicts Andela's flow chart to classify fires into four types: deforestation, forest, small clearing and agricultural, and savanna and grasland fires. It uses historic deforestation and fractional tree cover data to separate high-confidence deforestation fires from savanna and grassland fires.

Figure 2: Classification

Figure 2 highlights the study area in Colombia by shading in green on the map of South America. Black pixels indicate fires detected in 2003, which was one of the years with the highest number of fires in Colombia.

Figure 3: Study Area

Figure 3 displays the first classification results of the black pixels indicating fires detected in 2004, viewed from Figure 2. Red pixels indicate areas where fires occurred due to deforestation with high confidence, while black pixels require further classification. The majority of the region shows no significant deforestation fire activity except for a few instances in the western middle of the country.

Figure 4: Classification

Figure 4 The top figure displays remaining fires that were not classified in Figure 3. The bottom left figure shows fires resulting from the classification of small clearing or agriculture. The bottom right figure displays the high-confidence classification result of forest fires.

Figure 5: Fires due to deforestation

Figure 5 illustrates high-confidence deforestation fires across South America in 2012, with different colors indicating the month of ignition.
