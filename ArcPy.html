Moth Dataset

This animation provides a visual representation of how moth populations change over the course of a year, with different countries experiencing different patterns of growth and decline. Interestingly, some countries, such as China and Australia, show no change over time, while others experience more pronounced fluctuations. The frequency table is broken down by country and moth count, with yellow representing counts from 1-8, tan from 9-14, yellowish orange form 15-21, red from 22 counts to 27, and maroon red from 28-34 counts. The GIF serves as a useful tool for detecting changes in moth populations across a wide area.
